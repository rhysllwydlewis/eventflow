Event Decoration & Styling on a Budget

Create a stunning event atmosphere without overspending — practical styling tips for every budget

📚 7 min read • Updated March 2026

✍️ EventFlow Team 📅 Published: 8 March 2026

🎨 Establishing Your Style and Theme

Before spending a single penny on décor, spend time defining your theme and colour palette. Consistent styling across every element — from table centrepieces to welcome signage — creates a polished result even on a modest budget. A scattered mix of styles, however expensive, rarely looks as good as a cohesive, intentional scheme.

Choosing Your Colour Palette

Limit yourself to two or three complementary colours. This makes it far easier to shop consistently and ensures items from different suppliers don't clash. Popular UK wedding and event palettes in 2026 include:

  • Sage green, ivory, and terracotta
  • Navy blue, gold, and white
  • Blush pink, champagne, and grey
  • Burnt orange, cream, and deep burgundy (particularly popular for autumn events)
  • All-white or all-black for contemporary corporate events

Finding Inspiration

Build a mood board before you start purchasing anything. Pinterest is the most popular tool for this, but Instagram and wedding blogs are equally useful. Save images that appeal to you and look for common threads — do you favour greenery-heavy florals, minimalist tablescapes, or abundant candlelight? That pattern will guide your purchasing decisions.

Practical tip: Visit your venue before finalising your décor scheme. A barn with exposed oak beams needs a very different approach to a contemporary hotel ballroom. Work with what the space already offers rather than against it.

🔨 DIY vs Hiring a Stylist

The DIY vs professional styling decision is about more than just cost — it's about time, skill, and the stress you're willing to take on in the lead-up to your event.

When DIY Makes Sense

  • You have creative confidence and experience with craft projects
  • You have a team of willing helpers (bridesmaids, family members) to assist with setup
  • Your venue allows DIY setup the day before rather than just on the morning
  • Your style is relatively simple — candles, greenery, and ribbon rather than structural installations

When to Hire a Stylist

  • You want a complex installation (flower wall, ceiling drapes, bespoke arch)
  • You're worried about the setup timeline on the day
  • You lack the time or inclination to source and transport large quantities of items
  • You want a guaranteed professional finish without the stress

A partial approach works well for many couples: hire a stylist for the wow-factor elements (arch, top table backdrop) and DIY the smaller details (place cards, favours, centrepiece finishing touches).

🌸 Centrepiece Ideas for Every Budget

Centrepieces are the most visible element of your table styling and often where décor budgets are spent most heavily. Here's how to create impact at different price points.

Under £20 per Table

  • Pillar candles in varying heights on a mirrored tile, surrounded by seasonal foliage from a garden centre
  • Jam jars filled with wildflowers tied with hessian twine
  • Potted herbs (rosemary, lavender, thyme) — guests can take these home as favours
  • Books stacked with a single stem in a bud vase on top

£20–£60 per Table

  • Bud vase clusters with mixed seasonal stems from a local flower market
  • A lantern with candles surrounded by eucalyptus and dried pampas grass
  • Hire low cylinder vases filled with flowers from a florist on a DIY basis

£60+ per Table

  • Tall statement arrangements in gold or silver geometric vases
  • Lush garden-style arrangements with garden roses, sweet peas, and trailing vines
  • Candelabra draped with hanging amaranthus or orchids

Seasonal Flowers Save Money

UK-grown seasonal flowers are significantly cheaper than imported varieties. In summer, British roses, sweet peas, larkspur, and dahlias are abundant and affordable. In winter, lean into amaryllis, hellebores, ivy, and eucalyptus. Discuss seasonal options with your florist — they'll always be able to suggest beautiful alternatives to out-of-season blooms.

💡 Lighting as a Décor Tool

Lighting is one of the highest-impact and most cost-effective ways to transform a space. A venue that looks ordinary in fluorescent light becomes magical with the right atmospheric lighting.

Types of Lighting to Consider

  • Festoon lights: Warm Edison bulbs strung across a marquee or outdoor space create instant ambience. Hire from £150–£400 depending on the area to cover.
  • Uplighters: Coloured LED uplighters placed around the room wash walls in your chosen colour. Hire typically costs £30–£50 per unit; you'll usually need 10–20 for a medium-sized room.
  • Fairy lights: Affordable and versatile — drape them around arches, thread them through foliage, or use them to back a seating plan.
  • Candles: Nothing beats real candlelight for warmth and romance. Use pillar candles, votives, and taper candles across all tables. Always check your venue's fire safety policy first.
  • Neon signs: Hire a custom neon sign with your names, initials, or a favourite phrase. Typical hire cost: £100–£300.

♻️ Hire, Buy Second-Hand, or Go Sustainable

Hiring vs Buying

For large quantities of items you'll use once — vases, candelabra, charger plates, chair sashes — hiring is almost always cheaper than buying outright. Décor hire companies across the UK offer a wide range of items at a fraction of the retail price.

Second-Hand Décor via the Marketplace

The EventFlow Marketplace is an excellent place to find second-hand event décor at a fraction of the retail price. Many couples sell their wedding décor immediately after the event, meaning you can find nearly-new items — arches, lanterns, table runners, charger plates — for 20–40% of their original cost. Similarly, after your event you can list your items on the marketplace to recoup some of your spend.

Sustainable Decoration Choices

  • Choose biodegradable confetti (dried flower petals) over plastic versions — many UK venues now ban plastic confetti entirely
  • Opt for potted plants over cut flowers where possible
  • Use recyclable or compostable tableware for casual outdoor events
  • Choose candles made from soy or beeswax rather than paraffin
  • Donate or resell all décor items after the event rather than disposing of them

Balloon Displays vs Flower Walls

Both are popular photo-op backdrops at UK events. A professional balloon installation (organic garland arch) typically costs £200–£600 installed. A fresh flower wall is more expensive (£400–£1,200) but looks extraordinary in photographs. Dried flower walls are a more sustainable and affordable alternative at £150–£400 for hire. Always clarify whether teardown is included in the hire price.
