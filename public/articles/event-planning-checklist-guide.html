Event Planning Checklist: Week-by-Week Countdown

Stay on top of every detail with our comprehensive week-by-week countdown. From booking your venue 12 months out to your final day-before checks.

📚 8 min read • Updated March 2026

✍️ EventFlow Team 📅 Published: 12 March 2026

📅 12+ Months Before

This is the big-picture phase. Focus on the non-negotiables that will shape everything else: venue, date, and budget.

  • Agree on a rough budget and who is contributing what
  • Decide on the overall vision and style (formal, relaxed, outdoor, destination)
  • Start your guest list — even a rough headcount helps with venue shortlisting
  • Research and visit venues; enquire about availability for your preferred dates
  • Book your venue as soon as you've decided — popular venues fill up 12–18 months in advance
  • Begin researching photographers and videographers — the best ones book up earliest
  • Notify your local register office if you plan a civil ceremony (notice of marriage must be given at least 28 days in advance, but book your appointment well ahead)
  • Take out wedding or event insurance immediately after booking your venue
  • Set up a dedicated email address for supplier communications
  • Create a shared planning folder (Google Drive, Notion, or similar) to store quotes and contracts

📅 9–12 Months Before

With your venue secured, now build the supplier team around it.

  • Book your photographer and videographer
  • Book your main evening entertainment (live band or DJ)
  • Start researching caterers if your venue uses external caterers
  • Send save-the-dates to guests (essential if many are travelling from afar)
  • Start dress and suit shopping — allow 6–9 months for made-to-order or alterations
  • Research and shortlist florists, cake makers, and hair and make-up artists
  • Start a honeymoon research list if you're planning one
  • Decide whether to hire a wedding or event planner or coordinator
  • Set up a wedding website with your key details

📅 6–9 Months Before

Decisions move from the big picture to the detail. Start confirming your creative suppliers.

  • Book your florist
  • Book your cake maker (tastings usually happen at this stage)
  • Book ceremony entertainment (string quartet, acoustic act, or organist)
  • Book your hair and make-up artist
  • Order or finalise your wedding dress; schedule first fitting
  • Choose and book bridesmaids' or groomsmen's outfits
  • Plan and book your honeymoon; arrange visas if needed
  • Book overnight accommodation for yourselves on the wedding night
  • Research and book transport (wedding car, shuttle bus for guests)
  • Begin designing stationery (invitations, orders of service, menus, place cards)
  • Plan ceremony structure and any readings or music

📅 3–6 Months Before

The detail phase begins. Send formal invitations and start nailing down the fine print.

  • Send wedding invitations (aim for 3 months before the event)
  • Set an RSVP deadline (6–8 weeks before is standard)
  • Book additional entertainment and add-ons (photo booth, magician, casino tables)
  • Arrange accommodation blocks for guests if needed
  • Plan the seating chart in outline once RSVPs start returning
  • Book rehearsal dinner or pre-event gathering if planned
  • Purchase wedding rings
  • Arrange gift list or alternative (charity, experience fund, etc.)
  • Schedule final dress fittings
  • Arrange groom's and groomsmen's suits if hiring
  • Confirm menu choices with your caterer
  • Arrange wedding favours
  • Plan décor details (centrepieces, table settings, signage)

Budget check: This is a good point to review your spending against your original budget. Any savings made now give you flexibility for last-minute extras later.

📅 1–3 Months Before

Chase RSVPs, finalise numbers, and move into logistics mode.

  • Chase outstanding RSVPs when your deadline passes
  • Finalise and submit guest numbers to your venue and caterer
  • Complete the seating plan
  • Order place cards and finalise menus
  • Brief all suppliers with a detailed schedule — ceremony time, arrival time, location, parking
  • Confirm final details with your photographer (shot list, key moments, group photo list)
  • Write or finalise speeches
  • Confirm your music choices with your band, DJ, and ceremony musician
  • Arrange travel to and from the venue for yourselves
  • Notify your insurance if guest numbers or venue details have changed
  • Prepare information packs for guests (directions, parking, accommodation, nearest taxi numbers)

📅 2–4 Weeks Before

Final confirmations and logistical prep. The planning is largely done — now it's about execution.

  • Confirm all supplier arrival times, addresses, and contacts in a single master document
  • Designate a point-of-contact for each supplier on the day (ideally not the couple)
  • Send final payment to suppliers as contracts require
  • Pick up wedding dress after final fitting
  • Arrange final hair and make-up trials if not already done
  • Confirm hen and stag do arrangements if planned for this period
  • Prepare gratuity envelopes for suppliers you wish to tip
  • Pack an emergency kit (safety pins, stain remover, pain relief, plasters)
  • Arrange care for pets or children if needed
  • Brief your wedding party on their roles and the running order

📅 The Week Before

  • Confirm all suppliers one final time by phone or message
  • Deliver items to the venue if they permit it (favours, décor, place cards)
  • Pack for the honeymoon if departing shortly after
  • Attend the rehearsal (for civil ceremonies or those with specific choreography)
  • Get plenty of rest — this week can be hectic, so protect your energy
  • Hand the master supplier schedule to your designated on-the-day coordinator or trusted friend
  • Confirm reception seating plan is displayed or ready to display
  • Charge all devices (phone, camera battery packs)
  • Prepare payment for any suppliers being paid on the day

📅 Day Before & Day Of

The Day Before

  • Collect any final items (wedding rings, buttonholes, cake)
  • Set up the venue if access is permitted
  • Enjoy your pre-wedding gathering or rehearsal dinner
  • Get an early night — sleep is the best pre-wedding preparation
  • Lay out everything you need for the morning in order

On the Day

  • Have a proper breakfast — it's going to be a long, wonderful day
  • Allow extra time for hair and make-up
  • Ensure your on-the-day coordinator has the master supplier list
  • Hand rings and any ceremony items to your best man or maid of honour
  • Trust that everything is in hand — you've planned meticulously, and it's time to enjoy it
