Hiring Suppliers on EventFlow: A First-Timer's Guide

Finding and booking the right suppliers can feel overwhelming — but EventFlow makes it simple. Here's a step-by-step guide to finding, vetting, and booking suppliers on the platform.

📚 7 min read • Updated March 2026

✍️ EventFlow Team 📅 Published: 15 March 2026

🚀 Getting Started

If you're new to EventFlow, welcome. The platform is designed to make supplier discovery and booking as straightforward as possible — whether you're planning a small birthday party or a 200-guest wedding.

Creating Your Account

Start by creating a free account at event-flow.co.uk/auth. You'll be asked for your name and email address, and you can sign up in under a minute. There's no subscription fee to browse suppliers or send enquiries — you only need to upgrade to a premium plan if you want access to advanced planning tools, unlimited messaging, and priority support.

Setting Up Your Event Profile

Once logged in, head to your dashboard and create an event profile. This is where you enter the key details of your event: the date, approximate location, guest numbers, and event type (wedding, corporate event, birthday, and so on). Setting up a profile isn't mandatory to browse, but it enables the platform to surface the most relevant suppliers for your needs and allows you to send pre-filled enquiries to multiple suppliers simultaneously.

Tip: Be as specific as possible in your event profile. Suppliers with availability on your exact date and in your location will appear higher in your search results.

🔍 Searching for Suppliers

EventFlow lists hundreds of verified suppliers across the UK, covering every category you're likely to need — from photographers and caterers to florists, bands, and marquee hire companies.

Using Filters Effectively

The supplier search page offers several filters to help you narrow down your results quickly:

  • Category: Select the type of supplier you need (e.g., Photography, Catering, Entertainment, Florist, Venue).
  • Location: Search by town, city, or postcode. You can also set a radius to see suppliers willing to travel to your event location.
  • Price range: Filter by starting price or package cost to stay within budget. Note that many suppliers list indicative prices — always confirm final pricing directly with the supplier.
  • Availability: If you've set an event date in your profile, you can filter for suppliers who have indicated availability on that date. This is one of the most useful features for narrowing down a shortlist quickly.
  • Verified badge: Filter to show only suppliers who have completed EventFlow's verification process, which includes identity confirmation, insurance checks, and review validation.

👁️ Reading Supplier Profiles

Every supplier on EventFlow has a detailed profile page. Knowing what to look for will save you time and help you make better decisions.

Portfolio and Photos

A supplier's portfolio is your most important reference point. Look for consistency in quality and style across multiple events — not just one or two standout images. A photographer, for example, should be able to show you work from a variety of venues and lighting conditions, not just perfectly lit outdoor shoots.

Reviews and Ratings

EventFlow reviews are submitted by verified customers — only people who have genuinely booked through the platform can leave a review. Read beyond the star rating: look at how recent the reviews are, whether the supplier responds to feedback (positive or negative), and whether the specifics mentioned in reviews match what you're looking for.

The Verified Badge

The EventFlow Verified badge indicates that a supplier has passed our identity checks, provided proof of appropriate insurance (including public liability), and had their reviews validated. It's a baseline indicator of legitimacy — though we always recommend doing your own due diligence as well.

Response Time

Each supplier profile shows their average response time to enquiries. If a supplier typically replies within a few hours, you'll get your questions answered quickly. A slow average response time isn't necessarily a red flag — busy suppliers with high demand may take longer — but it's useful context when you're working to a tight decision deadline.

⚖️ Comparing Packages and Prices

Most suppliers offer multiple packages at different price points. Before comparing, make sure you're comparing like-for-like:

  • Does the package include travel costs, or are those added separately?
  • Is VAT included in the listed price? (Not all suppliers are VAT-registered.)
  • What is the duration of service — and what happens if your event runs over?
  • Are setup and breakdown times included in the hire period?
  • What equipment does the supplier provide, and what do you need to source separately?

You can save supplier profiles to your shortlist by clicking the bookmark icon on any profile page. Your shortlisted suppliers are then visible from your dashboard, making it easy to compare options side by side.

Don't just go for the cheapest: The lowest price doesn't always represent the best value. A supplier with slightly higher prices who responds promptly, has glowing reviews, and offers a detailed contract is often worth the premium.

💬 Messaging Suppliers

Once you've identified suppliers you'd like to know more about, use the EventFlow messaging system to make contact. All communication is kept within the platform until you're ready to proceed — this protects both parties and gives you a clear record of everything discussed.

What to Include in Your First Message

A well-crafted first message will get you a better, faster response. Include:

  • Event date: Be specific — day, month, and year.
  • Venue or location: Even an approximate town is helpful for suppliers to assess travel requirements.
  • Guest count: Approximate numbers are fine at this stage.
  • Budget: You don't need to be precise, but giving a ballpark figure (e.g., "we're looking to spend around £800–£1,200") helps suppliers recommend appropriate packages and avoids wasted time for both parties.
  • Specific requirements: Any unusual requests or constraints — for example, a tight noise curfew, a venue without a kitchen, or a specific dietary requirement for a caterer — are worth mentioning upfront.
  • A specific question: Ending your message with a direct question ("Do you have availability on this date?" or "Would you be able to provide a quote for X?") makes it easy for the supplier to respond constructively.

✅ Making Your Decision

Once you've received quotes and had initial conversations, it's time to narrow down to your preferred supplier. A few things to do before committing:

Checking References

Don't be shy about asking for references from previous clients — especially for higher-value bookings like wedding caterers or photographers. A reputable supplier will expect this request and respond positively. If a supplier is reluctant to provide references, treat that as a warning sign.

Reviewing the Contract

Always ask for a written contract before paying any deposit. Review it carefully for the event date, start and finish times, the specific services included, the total price and payment schedule, cancellation terms (for both sides), and what happens in the event of supplier illness or equipment failure. If anything is missing or unclear, ask for amendments in writing before signing.

Deposits

A deposit of 20–30% of the total booking value is standard across the UK events industry. Be cautious of suppliers asking for full payment upfront — this is unusual and carries greater financial risk if something goes wrong. Pay deposits by bank transfer or credit card rather than cash, so you have a clear record and potential recourse through your bank.

📁 After Booking

Once your booking is confirmed and your deposit is paid, use the EventFlow dashboard to keep everything organised:

  • Track payments: Log your deposit and any scheduled payment milestones so nothing slips through the cracks.
  • Keep communication in the platform: Where possible, continue using EventFlow messaging for supplier communications. This keeps everything in one place and provides a useful record if any disputes arise.
  • Set reminders: Add payment due dates and supplier briefing deadlines to your event timeline.
  • Confirm details closer to the day: Most suppliers appreciate a confirmation call or message two to four weeks before the event to confirm timings, access arrangements, and any last-minute changes.
  • Leave a review after the event: Reviews help other couples and event planners make informed decisions. If a supplier delivered excellent service, take a few minutes to share your experience on EventFlow — it makes a real difference to their business.
