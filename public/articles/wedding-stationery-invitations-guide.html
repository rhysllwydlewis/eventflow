Wedding Stationery & Invitations Guide

Everything from save-the-dates to thank-you cards — a complete guide to wedding stationery in the UK

📚 6 min read • Updated March 2026

✍️ EventFlow Team 📅 Published: 10 March 2026

📋 The Full Wedding Stationery Suite

Wedding stationery isn't just the invitation — it's every piece of printed (or digital) communication that makes up your guests' experience from the moment you announce your wedding to the thank-you card they receive weeks later.

Before the Wedding

  • Save-the-date cards: A brief, informal announcement sent 9–12 months before the wedding, particularly important if you're marrying on a bank holiday weekend or expecting many guests to travel.
  • Invitations: The main event. Should include all essential information and set the tone and style for the wedding.
  • Information cards: Additional inserts covering accommodation options, travel directions, dress code, wedding website URL, and gift list details.
  • RSVP cards: A pre-addressed reply card (with a stamp, if you're being considerate) so guests can confirm attendance and dietary requirements easily.

On the Day

  • Order of service: Used during the ceremony to guide guests through readings, hymns, and the programme of events.
  • Menus: Placed at each setting to show the wedding breakfast choices. Personalised with guest names doubles as a place card.
  • Table plan: Displayed at the entrance to the dining room, showing guests which table they are seated at.
  • Place cards: Placed at each seat to direct guests to their exact position at the table.
  • Signage: Welcome signs, bar menus, seating plan frames, directional signs, and any bespoke display boards.

After the Wedding

  • Thank-you cards: Sent within six to eight weeks of the wedding to thank guests for attending and for any gifts received. Personalise each one with a specific mention of the gift and a warm memory from the day.

📅 UK Timeline for Sending Invitations

Timing is everything with wedding stationery. Send too early and guests may lose the invitation; leave it too late and people have made other plans.

  • 12–18 months before: Commission your stationery designer or begin sourcing DIY materials
  • 9–12 months before: Send save-the-date cards (earlier for international guests or bank holiday weekends)
  • 6–8 months before: Send formal invitations to all-day guests
  • 4–6 months before: Send evening-only invitations
  • RSVP deadline: Set this 6–8 weeks before the wedding to allow time to finalise catering numbers and seating
  • 2–3 weeks before: Chase non-responders and finalise all day-of stationery (menus, place cards, table plan)
  • 6–8 weeks after: Send thank-you cards

✉️ What to Include on a Wedding Invitation

A well-written invitation gives guests everything they need to attend confidently. Don't assume they'll visit your wedding website for essential information — include the key details on the invitation itself.

Essential Information

  • Full names of the couple being married
  • The date (day, date, and year in full — not just the date)
  • Ceremony start time and venue name and address (including postcode)
  • Reception venue name and address (if different from ceremony)
  • RSVP deadline and how to respond
  • Whether evening-only guests are invited to the ceremony or just the reception

Optional but Helpful Additions

  • Dress code (black tie, morning dress, smart casual, etc.)
  • Wedding website URL
  • Gift list information (tastefully worded)
  • Confirmation of whether children are welcome
  • Parking or transport information

Addressing Etiquette

Address envelopes formally in the UK: "Mr and Mrs James Taylor" or "Dr Sarah Green and Mr Tom Green." For guests invited with a plus one whose name you don't know, use "Miss Anna Brown and Guest." Include inner envelopes (listing exactly who is invited from each household) if your invitation includes ambiguity about whether children are included.

🖨️ Printed vs Digital Invitations

The Case for Printed Invitations

Printed invitations feel special in a way that a digital message cannot replicate. Many guests keep beautiful wedding invitations as a memento. Printed stationery also reaches guests of all ages reliably, regardless of their comfort with technology.

The cost of a complete printed invitation suite — invitation, information card, RSVP card, and envelope — typically ranges from £2–£8 per guest depending on printing method and paper quality. Luxury finishes (foil, letterpress, vellum overlays) can push costs higher.

The Case for Digital Invitations

Digital invitations and wedding websites are increasingly popular, particularly for eco-conscious couples or those with tight stationery budgets. Platforms allow RSVP tracking, dietary requirement collection, and real-time updates all in one place. Many couples use a hybrid approach: a brief printed card with a QR code directing guests to a wedding website for full details.

Postage and Delivery

Weigh your invitation suite fully assembled before purchasing stamps — additional inserts, thick cardstock, and unusual envelope shapes (square, A5) often require extra postage. Send one test invitation to yourself before posting the full batch to check postage, print quality, and that everything is in order inside the envelope.

Pro tip: Always send a test invitation to yourself before posting the full batch. This lets you check the postage weight, print quality, and that all inserts fit neatly inside the envelope — a small step that can save you from reprinting dozens of suites.

🌿 Eco-Friendly and Budget-Friendly Options

Sustainable Paper Choices

  • FSC-certified paper from responsibly managed forests
  • Recycled or cotton-rag paper (often has a beautiful texture)
  • Seed paper — guests can plant the invitation after the wedding
  • Soy-based or water-based inks rather than solvent inks

DIY Stationery

If you're creative and have time, DIY stationery can significantly reduce costs. Design tools like Canva offer hundreds of beautiful wedding invitation templates that can be personalised and printed at home or at a local print shop. Factor in the cost of quality card stock, envelopes, and your time when comparing DIY costs against a designer.

Finding Templates and Second-Hand Items on the Marketplace

The EventFlow Marketplace is a great place to find customisable stationery templates from designers, or to purchase items like table plan frames, signage boards, and envelope wax seal kits second-hand from couples who have recently married. These items are often sold at a fraction of their original cost and may only have been used once.

Managing Lost Post

Always keep a spreadsheet of who has received an invitation. If an RSVP deadline passes with no response, follow up politely — it's common for invitation suites to go astray in the post, particularly in busy periods such as late November and early December. Keeping spare copies of invitations allows you to quickly resend without the delay of ordering reprints.

Money-saving tip: Order 10–15 spare invitation sets beyond your guest count. Reprints of small quantities are disproportionately expensive, and spares give you room to add late additions to the guest list, replace lost invitations, and keep one as a keepsake without stress.
