Choosing the Right Event Caterer: Questions to Ask

Know exactly what to ask before you sign the contract — a complete checklist for hiring a UK event caterer

📚 8 min read • Updated March 2026

✍️ EventFlow Team 📅 Published: 9 March 2026

🍽️ Types of Catering Service Style

Before approaching caterers, decide which service style suits your event. This shapes the per-head cost, the staffing required, and the overall atmosphere of the meal.

Sit-Down (Plated) Meal

The most formal option. Waitstaff serve each course to seated guests. Creates a structured, elegant dining experience. Works well for weddings, corporate dinners, and gala events. Typically costs £60–£150+ per head depending on menu complexity and staffing.

Buffet

A popular and cost-effective option for larger guest counts. Guests help themselves from a central display, which reduces staffing requirements. Works well for informal receptions and corporate events. Costs typically range from £35–£80 per head.

Food Stations

Interactive stations around the room — a carving station, a pasta bar, a cheese and charcuterie table. Creates a relaxed, sociable atmosphere. Great for cocktail receptions and evening parties.

Canapés and Bowl Food

Circulating canapés are ideal for drinks receptions. Bowl food (small, individual portions of a single dish) bridges the gap between canapés and a full meal — popular for cocktail-style events where guests remain standing.

Street Food and BBQ

Increasingly popular for outdoor summer weddings and corporate events. Typically more relaxed and budget-friendly (£20–£45 per head). Street food traders often bring their own equipment and require minimal catering infrastructure from the venue.

❓ Questions About Experience and References

A caterer's experience is your best assurance of quality. Don't be afraid to dig into their track record.

  1. How many events of our size and type have you catered? Ask for specific examples comparable to yours — a caterer excellent at intimate dinner parties may struggle with 200-person weddings.
  2. Can you provide references from recent clients? Always follow up with at least two references. Ask specifically about food quality, presentation, punctuality, and how they handled any issues on the day.
  3. Have you catered at our venue before? Familiarity with the kitchen facilities, load-in access, and venue rules is a significant advantage.
  4. Who will be the lead chef and on-site manager on our day? Ensure the person running the event is not just a junior member of staff — and meet them in advance if possible.
  5. What happens if you are unable to fulfil the booking? A reputable caterer will have a clear plan for illness, emergency, or business disruption.

🥗 Questions About Menus and Dietary Requirements

Food is one of the most memorable parts of any event — for good reasons or bad. Ask these questions to ensure your guests are well fed and safe.

Menu Flexibility

  1. Can we customise the menu, or do you work from set packages?
  2. Are you able to accommodate a seasonal or locally sourced menu?
  3. Do you offer a children's menu, and at what cost?
  4. Can we arrange a tasting session before we confirm the booking?

Dietary Requirements and Allergens

  1. How do you handle the 14 major allergens as specified under UK food law?
  2. Can you provide fully vegan, vegetarian, halal, and kosher options?
  3. How do you prevent cross-contamination in the kitchen for guests with serious allergies?
  4. Will guests' dietary requirements be communicated to serving staff so the correct plate reaches the correct guest?

Legal requirement: Under the Food Information to Consumers Regulation (UK FIC), caterers must be able to provide allergen information for every dish they serve. Any caterer who cannot provide this should not be hired.

📋 Questions About Licensing and Food Safety

Food safety and licensing are non-negotiable. A professional caterer will have all of these in order and be happy to share them with you.

  1. What is your Food Hygiene Rating? UK caterers are rated 0–5 by their local authority. Aim for a rating of 4 or 5, and treat anything below 3 with caution. You can check ratings on the Food Standards Agency website.
  2. Do you hold a Premises Licence or a Temporary Event Notice (TEN) for serving alcohol at our event? If the caterer is supplying alcohol, they must be appropriately licensed.
  3. Do you hold Public Liability Insurance? A minimum of £2 million coverage is standard. Request a copy of the certificate before signing the contract.
  4. Are all staff members trained in food hygiene? Ask whether staff hold a Level 2 Food Hygiene certificate as a minimum.

👨‍🍳 Questions About Staffing and Service

The quality of service on the day is as important as the food itself. Slow, inattentive, or unprofessional service ruins the experience regardless of how good the menu is.

  1. What is your staff-to-guest ratio? For a sit-down meal, one server per 10–12 guests is the UK industry standard. Buffets can operate with fewer staff, typically one per 20–25 guests.
  2. What will your staff wear? Ensure it matches the formality of your event.
  3. Who coordinates the timeline with our venue and other suppliers on the day? Someone needs to own the catering schedule — confirm this is the caterer's responsibility.
  4. How do you handle a service delay? Ask for their contingency plan if the kitchen runs behind.
  5. Do you include a dedicated point of contact throughout the day? You should not have to manage caterers on your wedding day — ensure a senior member of their team is the liaison.

💰 Questions About Costs, Contracts, and the Day Itself

What's Included in the Quote?

Catering quotes vary enormously in what they include. Always confirm whether the following are included or charged as extras:

  • Linen (tablecloths, napkins)
  • Crockery, cutlery, and glassware
  • Serving equipment and chafing dishes
  • Staff travel and accommodation
  • Set-up and clearing time
  • Cake cutting and serving
  • Evening food (if separate from the main meal)
  • Corkage fees if you're supplying your own wine

Extra Charges to Watch For

  • Gratuity or service charge — often added as a percentage of the total bill
  • Overtime charges if the event runs past the agreed end time
  • Minimum guest numbers — some caterers have a minimum spend or headcount
  • VAT — confirm whether quotes are inclusive or exclusive of VAT

Contract and Payment Terms

  • Deposit (typically 20–30%) payable on booking
  • Final guest numbers deadline — usually 2–4 weeks before the event
  • Final balance due date — often 2–4 weeks before the event
  • Cancellation terms: what is refundable and at what point
  • Force majeure clause covering unexpected events
