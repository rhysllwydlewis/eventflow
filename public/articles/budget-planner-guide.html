How to Use EventFlow's Budget Planner

Set your budget, track spending, and manage supplier payments — all in one place

📚 6 min read • Updated March 2026

✍️ EventFlow Team 📅 Published: 7 March 2026

🚀 Getting Started with the Budget Planner

EventFlow's Budget Planner is designed to give you a clear, real-time picture of your event finances from the first deposit to the final balance. It removes the need for complicated spreadsheets and keeps all of your financial information in one secure place.

To access the Budget Planner, log in to your EventFlow account and navigate to your event dashboard. Select your event and click Budget in the left-hand menu. If you haven't created an event yet, head to Plan an Event to get started.

Step 1: Set Your Total Budget

The first thing the planner asks for is your overall event budget. Enter the maximum amount you are willing to spend across all categories. This becomes your ceiling — the planner will track how much of it you've allocated and spent as you add items.

If you're splitting costs (for example, contributions from both sets of parents at a wedding), enter the total combined figure and note the contributions separately in the notes field.

Step 2: Add Budget Categories

The planner comes pre-loaded with the most common event categories, and you can add custom ones as needed. Standard categories include:

  • Venue hire
  • Catering and beverages
  • Photography and videography
  • Entertainment (band, DJ, photo booth)
  • Flowers and décor
  • Stationery and signage
  • Transport (guest buses, wedding car)
  • Attire (dress, suit, accessories)
  • Hair and beauty
  • Cake
  • Accommodation (for couple and/or guests)
  • Honeymoon deposit
  • Contingency (we recommend 10% of your total budget)

Always include a contingency category of at least 10% of your total budget. Unexpected costs — overtime charges, last-minute additions, items damaged on the day — are almost inevitable.

💳 Tracking Deposits and Final Payments

One of the Budget Planner's most useful features is its ability to track the split between deposits paid and balances outstanding.

Adding a Supplier Payment

  1. Open a budget category and click Add payment
  2. Enter the supplier name and total agreed price
  3. Mark the deposit as paid, entering the amount and date
  4. Set a reminder for the final balance due date
  5. When the balance is paid, mark it as complete

Deposit Due Dates

The planner displays an upcoming payments calendar so you can see at a glance which balances are due in the coming weeks. This is particularly useful in the final two to three months before your event when multiple supplier balances often fall due simultaneously.

Linking Payments to Suppliers

If you've booked a supplier through EventFlow's supplier directory, you can link their entry directly to the budget line item. This means you can jump straight to their contact details or quote without leaving the budget screen.

📊 Generating Budget Reports

The Budget Planner gives you two key summary views at all times:

  • Allocated vs total budget: How much of your budget has been committed to suppliers (including amounts not yet paid)
  • Spent vs allocated: How much you have actually paid out versus what is still outstanding

Category Breakdown

The category breakdown view shows you exactly where your money is going in a visual chart format. This is useful for identifying areas where you've over-allocated and may need to trim, or where you have room to add something extra.

Exporting Your Budget

You can export the full budget breakdown as a PDF or CSV file at any time. This is helpful for sharing with a partner, parents contributing to costs, or a wedding planner who needs an overview of your financial position.

💡 Tips for Saving Money

The Budget Planner doesn't just track spending — used well, it can actively help you save money.

Set Category Limits Before Quoting

Decide your maximum spend for each category before you start approaching suppliers. Once you have a figure in mind, it's much easier to assess whether a quote represents good value — and to negotiate confidently.

Compare Multiple Quotes

For each category, get at least two or three quotes from different suppliers. The planner lets you add multiple quote entries per category so you can compare them side by side before committing.

Watch the Contingency Carefully

Never dip into your contingency to fund planned expenses — it exists only for genuine unexpected costs. If you find you're tempted to use it, that's a sign to look at reducing spend elsewhere.

Common Budget Mistakes to Avoid

  • Forgetting VAT — many supplier quotes are ex-VAT, which adds 20% to the headline price
  • Not accounting for gratuities and service charges
  • Overlooking one-off costs: postage for invitations, dress alterations, transport to the venue for setup
  • Setting a contingency that's too small (less than 5% of total budget)
  • Failing to track deposits, meaning balances due come as a surprise

🔗 Integrating with Your Planning Timeline

The Budget Planner works best when used alongside EventFlow's planning checklist and timeline tools. Budget items can be linked to timeline milestones — for example, the stationery budget line can be connected to the "Send invitations" task, so you're reminded to finalise costs at the right point in your planning journey.

Payment Reminders

Set payment reminders for every supplier balance due date. EventFlow will notify you by email and on-platform notification in advance of each payment, giving you time to arrange funds without a last-minute scramble.

Post-Event Review

After your event, the completed budget acts as a useful record — particularly for any insurance claims, tax purposes (for corporate events), or simply for helping friends who are planning their own events in the future.
