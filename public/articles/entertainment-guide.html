How to Choose Your Wedding Entertainment

From live bands to photo booths, find the entertainment that will make your wedding day truly unforgettable

📚 8 min read • Updated March 2026

✍️ EventFlow Team 📅 Published: 5 March 2026

🎵 Types of Wedding Entertainment

Wedding entertainment sets the mood from the moment guests arrive to the final song of the evening. With so many options available across the UK, it helps to understand what each type offers before you start comparing prices and availability.

Live Band vs DJ

This is the most common decision couples face. A live band delivers energy, personality, and a uniquely memorable atmosphere — there's nothing quite like watching musicians perform in the room. Bands typically cost between £1,500 and £5,000 for a UK wedding, depending on size and experience.

A professional DJ, on the other hand, offers flexibility, a wider range of music, and generally lower costs (£500–£1,500). A great DJ reads the room brilliantly and can transition seamlessly between genres to keep the dance floor full all evening.

Many couples opt for both: a live band for the first few hours, followed by a DJ to close out the night.

Acoustic Acts

A solo singer or acoustic duo is perfect for the drinks reception or wedding breakfast. They create a warm, intimate atmosphere without overwhelming conversation. Expect to pay £300–£800 for a two-hour set.

Interactive Entertainment

  • Photo booths — A perennial favourite. Guests love them, and you get a ready-made memento. Typical hire cost: £500–£900 for four hours.
  • Magicians — Close-up magic during the drinks reception keeps guests entertained while you have photographs taken. Expect to pay £300–£600.
  • Caricaturists — A brilliant conversation starter; guests take home a personalised piece of art. Costs range from £250–£500.
  • Casino tables — Fun money casinos add a glamorous twist to the evening reception. A two-table hire typically costs £600–£1,200.
  • Photo booths (360°) — The latest trend; a rotating arm captures slow-motion video clips guests can instantly share. Budget around £700–£1,200.

💰 Budgeting for Entertainment

Entertainment typically accounts for 8–12% of the total wedding budget. For an average UK wedding spend of around £20,000, that's roughly £1,600–£2,400.

Typical Cost Ranges

  • Live band (4-piece): £2,000–£4,500
  • Wedding DJ: £500–£1,500
  • Acoustic duo: £400–£900
  • Photo booth hire: £500–£900
  • Close-up magician: £300–£600
  • Caricaturist: £250–£500
  • Fun casino (2 tables): £600–£1,200

Money-saving tip: Book entertainment as a package where possible. Many suppliers offer a discount if you hire a DJ and photo booth together, or if you book them as part of a venue's preferred supplier list.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

  • Travel and accommodation for suppliers outside your local area
  • Additional set time beyond contracted hours
  • PA system hire if the venue doesn't provide one
  • Lighting rigs — often sold separately by bands
  • Gratuities (not mandatory, but appreciated for exceptional performances)

⏰ Timing Entertainment Throughout the Day

Good entertainment keeps guests engaged at every stage, not just during the evening reception.

Ceremony (if applicable)

A string quartet, harpist, or acoustic singer for the ceremony processional, signing of the register, and recessional adds a beautiful touch. Many musicians offer half-hour or one-hour sets designed specifically for ceremonies.

Drinks Reception (1–2 hours)

This is when guests mingle before the wedding breakfast. An acoustic act, close-up magician, or lawn games work perfectly. Avoid anything too loud that makes conversation difficult.

Wedding Breakfast

Background music (either a curated playlist or a quiet acoustic act) is ideal here. Many couples also use this time for speeches, which naturally fill the entertainment slot.

Evening Reception

This is where your main entertainment — band, DJ, or both — comes into its own. A typical evening reception runs from 7pm to midnight or 1am. Consider a photo booth and casino tables to keep guests entertained when they're off the dance floor.

Pro tip: Always check your venue's noise curfew before booking a band. Many UK venues enforce an 11pm or midnight cut-off for amplified music.

📋 Questions to Ask Entertainment Suppliers

Before you sign any contracts, make sure you have clear answers to the following:

About Experience & Availability

  1. How many weddings have you performed at, and can you share references or reviews?
  2. Have you performed at our venue before? Are you familiar with its layout and acoustics?
  3. Is the act we're seeing/hearing the exact act that will perform on our day?
  4. What happens if a band member is ill on the day?

About Logistics

  1. What are your setup and soundcheck requirements? How much time do you need?
  2. What equipment do you provide, and what does the venue need to supply?
  3. Do you have Public Liability Insurance? (This is non-negotiable.)
  4. Are all equipment PAT tested?
  5. Is the venue covered by a PRS/PPL music licence for recorded music? (Most venues hold this; check with your venue if unsure.)

About the Performance

  1. Can we request specific songs, and what is your policy on do-not-play lists?
  2. How do you handle the first dance? Do you learn the song specifically for us?
  3. How many breaks do you take, and what happens during those breaks?
  4. What do you wear? Can you match our colour scheme or dress code?

📅 Booking Timeline & Contracts

Popular wedding entertainers book up quickly — particularly for peak summer dates (May–September) and New Year's Eve.

Recommended Booking Timeline

  • 12–18 months before: Research and shortlist entertainment suppliers
  • 10–14 months before: Attend showcases or watch live performances if possible
  • 9–12 months before: Book your main evening entertainment (band or DJ)
  • 6–9 months before: Book ceremony and drinks reception entertainment
  • 3–6 months before: Book add-ons (photo booth, magician, casino tables)
  • 4–6 weeks before: Send final song lists, running order, and logistics brief

What Your Contract Should Include

  • Full supplier name, trading name, and contact details
  • Exact date, venue address, and performance times
  • Number of sets and break durations
  • Deposit amount and payment schedule
  • Cancellation and postponement policy
  • Force majeure clause
  • Confirmation of Public Liability Insurance
  • Any agreed song requests or setlist requirements

Deposit advice: A deposit of 20–30% is standard. Never pay the full amount upfront. Pay the balance only after a final pre-wedding call has confirmed all details.

♿ Accessibility Considerations

Great entertainment should be enjoyable for all of your guests. A few things to consider:

  • Volume levels: Ensure there is a quieter area — ideally in a separate room — where guests with sensory sensitivities, hearing aids, or young children can retreat if needed.
  • Lighting: Strobe lighting and rapid flash effects can affect guests with epilepsy or migraines. Always ask suppliers to disable these effects.
  • Stage access: If you're involving guests in any interactive entertainment (e.g., a game show-style act), ensure participation is fully optional.
  • Hearing loops: If your venue has a hearing loop system, make sure the entertainment supplier is aware and can connect to it where relevant.
  • Dietary and inclusive entertainment: For caricaturists and magicians who interact closely with guests, brief them on any guests who might find close-up interaction uncomfortable.
