Privacy Notice

Last updated: December 2024

Effective date: December 2024

EventFlow Limited ("EventFlow," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy and personal data. This Privacy Notice explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your personal information when you use our website and services at www.event-flow.co.uk and related platforms (collectively, the "Services").

This Privacy Notice applies to all users of our Services, including website visitors, customers planning events, and suppliers offering event-related services.

Organisation: EventFlow Limited
Registered in: England and Wales
Contact Email: admin@event-flow.co.uk

EventFlow is the data controller responsible for your personal data. If you have any questions about how we process your data or wish to exercise your rights, please contact us using the details above.

2. Personal Data We Collect

We collect and process different types of personal data depending on how you interact with our Services:

2.1 User Account Data

When you create an account with EventFlow, we collect:

  • Identity Data: Your name, email address
  • Account Credentials: Password (stored as a cryptographic hash using bcrypt), user ID
  • Account Information: User role (customer, supplier, or admin), verification status, account creation date
  • Usage Data: Last login date, marketing preferences, email verification status
  • Profile Data: User preferences, settings, saved content

2.2 Supplier Account Data

If you register as a supplier, we additionally collect:

  • Business Information: Supplier name, business description, contact details
  • Location Data: Business address, service areas, geographic coverage
  • Commercial Data: Packages offered, pricing information, availability
  • Performance Metrics: Reviews, ratings, health scores, approval status
  • Professional Status: Pro membership status, verification badges

2.3 Customer Planning Data

When you use EventFlow to plan events, we collect:

  • Event Plans: Event details, budgets, timelines, notes
  • Guest Information: Guest lists, invitation details (where provided)
  • Communication Data: Messages sent through our platform, conversation threads
  • Preference Data: Saved suppliers, comparisons, shortlists

2.4 Photos and Media

We collect and store:

  • Uploaded Images: Photos uploaded by suppliers or users
  • Media Metadata: Upload date, file information, associated supplier
  • Moderation Status: Approval status, rejection reasons, admin notes

2.5 Technical and Usage Data

We automatically collect certain information when you use our Services:

  • Device Information: IP address, browser type and version, device type
  • Usage Information: Pages visited, features used, time spent on the platform
  • Authentication Tokens: JWT tokens stored in HTTP-only cookies for session management

5. Data Storage and Security

5.1 Storage Infrastructure

Your data is stored using secure, professional-grade infrastructure:

  • Primary Database: MongoDB Atlas (cloud-based database service)
  • Photo Storage: AWS S3 (Amazon Web Services Simple Storage Service)
  • Data Location: Data centers located in the European Union and United Kingdom

5.2 Security Measures

We implement comprehensive technical and organizational security measures:

Authentication Security:

  • JSON Web Token (JWT) based authentication
  • HTTP-only, secure cookies to prevent XSS attacks
  • Password hashing using bcrypt with appropriate salt rounds
  • Minimum 32-character JWT secret keys
  • Rate limiting on authentication endpoints to prevent brute force attacks

Authorization and Access Control:

  • Role-based access control (RBAC) system
  • Separation of privileges between customers, suppliers, and administrators
  • Owner account protection preventing unauthorized changes
  • Admin-only restrictions on sensitive operations

Network Security:

  • HTTPS encryption (TLS/SSL) for all data in transit in production environments
  • Cross-Site Request Forgery (CSRF) protection middleware
  • Secure headers and content security policies
  • Regular security updates and patches

Data Protection:

  • Passwords never stored in plain text
  • Sensitive data excluded from data exports
  • Database encryption at rest
  • Secure backup procedures

10. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

Under the UK General Data Protection Regulation and the Data Protection Act 2018, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

10.1 Right to Access (Article 15)

You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.

How to exercise: Email admin@event-flow.co.uk with your request
Response time: Within 30 days
Format: JSON or CSV export of your data

10.2 Right to Rectification (Article 16)

You have the right to correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data.

How to exercise: Update your profile through the Settings page, or contact admin@event-flow.co.uk for assistance
Self-service: Most profile information can be updated directly in your account settings

10.3 Right to Erasure (Article 17) - "Right to be Forgotten"

You have the right to request deletion of your personal data in certain circumstances.

How to exercise: Contact admin@event-flow.co.uk to request account deletion
Scope: All personal data and associated content will be deleted
Exceptions: We may retain certain data where required by law or for legitimate legal purposes (e.g., audit logs)

10.4 Right to Data Portability (Article 20)

You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, machine-readable format.

How to exercise: Request a data export by emailing admin@event-flow.co.uk
Format: JSON (structured data)
Scope: User-provided data and platform-generated content

10.5 Right to Object (Article 21)

You have the right to object to certain types of data processing.

Marketing: Opt-out via marketing preferences in Settings or unsubscribe links in emails
Profiling: You can request to disable profiling features by contacting us

10.6 Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18)

You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances.

How to exercise: Contact admin@event-flow.co.uk with your request
Effect: We will suspend processing while your request is being considered

10.7 Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making (Article 22)

You have the right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing that significantly affect you.

Current status: EventFlow does not currently use fully automated decision-making processes
Smart features: Any AI-assisted features (e.g., smart tagging) are reviewed by administrators

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at admin@event-flow.co.uk. We may ask you to verify your identity before processing your request. We will respond to your request within 30 days, or notify you if we need additional time.

For more information about exercising your rights, visit our Data Subject Rights page.

11. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach that poses a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will:

  1. Contain the breach: Take immediate action to identify and stop the breach
  2. Assess the impact: Determine the scope, severity, and affected data
  3. Notify authorities: Report to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) within 72 hours if required
  4. Notify affected users: Inform you without undue delay if the breach poses a high risk to you
  5. Document the breach: Maintain records of the breach and our response
  6. Implement safeguards: Take corrective action to prevent future breaches

If we notify you of a data breach, we will provide information about what happened, what data was affected, and what steps you should take to protect yourself.

12. Children's Privacy

EventFlow is not intended for use by individuals under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 16. If you are under 16, please do not register for an account or provide any personal information to us.

If we become aware that we have collected personal data from a child under 16 without parental consent, we will take steps to delete that information as quickly as possible.

If you believe we have inadvertently collected information from a child under 16, please contact us immediately at admin@event-flow.co.uk.
