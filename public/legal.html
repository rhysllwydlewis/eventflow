‚Ü©Ô∏è Marketplace Returns & Consumer Rights

Understanding the Marketplace Model

EventFlow operates a peer-to-peer marketplace where transactions occur directly between buyers and sellers. This structure affects returns and consumer rights:

Consumer Rights Act 2015

For Trader Sales (Business Sellers):

  • Full Consumer Rights Act 2015 protections apply
  • 14-day cooling off period for distance sales (Distance Selling Regulations)
  • Goods must be as described, fit for purpose, and of satisfactory quality
  • Right to reject faulty items within 30 days for full refund
  • Right to repair or replacement for faults within 6 months

For Private Sales (Individual Sellers):

  • Limited statutory rights compared to trader sales
  • Items must still match description
  • No automatic 14-day cooling off period
  • Returns subject to seller's individual return policy
  • Seller must clearly state if they are private or business seller

How to Request a Return

  1. Contact Seller First: Message the seller directly through the EventFlow platform within 48 hours of receiving the item
  2. State Your Reason: Clearly explain why you want to return (not as described, faulty, changed mind, etc.)
  3. Provide Evidence: Include photos if item is damaged or not as described
  4. Wait for Response: Seller should respond within 48 hours
  5. Arrange Return: If seller agrees, arrange return shipping (who pays depends on reason for return)
  6. Platform Mediation: If seller doesn't respond or refuses unfairly, contact {{ABUSE_EMAIL}}

EventFlow's Limited Role

As a platform intermediary, EventFlow:

  • Does NOT: Handle returns directly between buyers and sellers
  • Does NOT: Process refunds for marketplace transactions
  • Does NOT: Act as party to the transaction
  • Does: Provide platform for communication between parties
  • Does: Mediate disputes when direct resolution fails
  • Does: Remove listings that breach policies
  • Does: Suspend or ban users who violate marketplace rules

Who Pays Return Shipping?

  • Item Faulty/Not as Described: Seller should pay return shipping
  • Change of Mind (Private Sale): Buyer typically pays unless seller's policy states otherwise
  • Change of Mind (Trader Sale): Buyer pays return shipping for cooling off period returns
  • Wrong Item Sent: Seller pays return shipping

UK Consumer Rights Resources

For independent advice on your consumer rights:

  • Citizens Advice Consumer Service: 0808 223 1133
  • Website: www.citizensadvice.org.uk/consumer/
  • Which? Consumer Rights: www.which.co.uk/consumer-rights
  • Trading Standards: Contact via Citizens Advice Consumer Service

Important: Always check whether a seller is trading as a business or private individual. Business sellers must comply with full Consumer Rights Act 2015. If unclear, ask the seller directly or contact our support team.

Important Information About Marketplace Payments:

  • EventFlow does NOT currently process payments directly for marketplace transactions
  • Payment arrangements are made peer-to-peer between buyers and sellers
  • We are NOT a party to financial transactions
  • We do NOT hold buyer or seller funds
  • Buyers and sellers communicate privately to arrange payment

Recommended Secure Payment Methods

We strongly recommend using payment methods with buyer protection:

Safety Guidelines

  1. Never Pay Outside the Agreed Method: If seller requests a different payment method after agreement, be suspicious
  2. Document Everything: Keep records of all communications and payment confirmations
  3. Use Platform Messaging: Keep discussions on EventFlow for dispute evidence
  4. Verify Seller Identity: Check seller ratings, reviews, and verification status
  5. Be Cautious of Pressure: Scammers often create urgency ("pay now or lose it")
  6. Check PayPal Type: Ensure "Goods & Services" not "Friends & Family"

Age Restriction - 18+ Only

EventFlow is restricted to users aged 18 years or older.

Why Age Restrictions Apply

  • Legal Contracts: Users must be of legal age to enter binding contracts
  • Financial Transactions: Marketplace buying/selling requires legal capacity
  • Event Planning: Many events involve age-restricted activities (alcohol, venues)
  • Data Protection: Special protections for children under GDPR/UK GDPR
  • Consumer Rights: Contract law requires parties to have legal capacity

Registration Requirements

When creating an account, users must:

  1. Confirm Age: Check "I confirm I am 18 years of age or older"
  2. Accurate Information: Provide truthful date of birth
  3. Accept Terms: Acknowledge age restriction in Terms of Use
  4. Parent/Guardian Consent: NOT applicable - no accounts for users under 18

Age-Restricted Items on Marketplace

Additional age restrictions apply to certain marketplace items:

  • Alcohol: 18+ (UK legal drinking age)
  • Tobacco/Vaping: 18+ (UK legal age)
  • Knives/Blades: 18+ (as per UK law)
  • Adult Content: 18+ (must be clearly marked)
  • Gambling-Related: 18+ (UK gambling age)

Sellers must: Clearly mark age-restricted items and verify buyer age before completing sale

Age Verification Process

Current verification measures:

  • Self-Declaration: Users confirm age at registration
  • Email Verification: Valid email address required
  • Behavioral Monitoring: Automated systems flag suspicious underage activity
  • Manual Review: Accounts flagged for age concerns are manually reviewed

Future enhancements under consideration:

  • ID Verification: Optional identity verification for marketplace sellers
  • Age Verification Services: Third-party age verification for high-value transactions
  • Enhanced Checks: Additional verification for age-restricted item purchases

Enforcement & Consequences

If we discover a user is under 18:

  1. Immediate Account Suspension: Account access is immediately disabled
  2. Transaction Review: Any active transactions are reviewed and may be cancelled
  3. Permanent Ban: User is permanently banned from creating future accounts
  4. Notification: Parent/guardian contacted if contact information available
  5. Data Deletion: Personal data deleted in accordance with GDPR (right to erasure)
